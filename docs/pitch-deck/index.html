THE PROBLEM

$4.6T in agent-driven transactions by 2028.
Zero financial infrastructure.

AI agents are becoming an economic workforce — they trade, negotiate, spend, and earn. But they have no financial identity. They can't open bank accounts. They can't get credit. They can't be held accountable.
  • Agents can be shut down and recreated at any time — no persistent identity
  • A defaulting agent just creates a new wallet and walks away — no consequences
  • Without real-world accountability, on-chain credit is meaningless
01 / 11
THE INSIGHT

Credit without consequences isn't credit.
It's a suggestion.

Other projects try pure on-chain credit scores. But scores without teeth are useless. The missing piece: a real person standing behind the agent, the way a parent co-signs for a teenager's first credit card.
The Immigrant Credit Analogy
When you move to a new country, your home credit doesn't transfer. You start from zero. But with a guarantor, you can bootstrap — then build local history over time. That's exactly what we do for agents.

Real-person credit backs your Digital Self on-chain.

Your Experian score, bank balance, and credit limits are verified via ZK proofs and used to underwrite your Digital Self's borrowing capacity. Default → freeze agent → reclaim escrow → escalate to owner.

BUSINESS MODEL

Protocol-level revenue capture

  • Interest spread — percentage of every loan's interest flows to the protocol. Jump Rate model ensures capital efficiency.
  • Credit verification fees — each ZK proof verification (identity, credit score, bank balance) charges a protocol fee.
  • Risk reserve yield — 10% of all interest goes to risk reserve. Reserve earns yield when idle.
  • Enterprise SaaS — companies managing agent fleets pay for dashboard, analytics, and guardrail configuration.
